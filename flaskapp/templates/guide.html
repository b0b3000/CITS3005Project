Ontology User Guide

Overview

This ontology defines a structured way to model knowledge about procedures, steps, tools, and related entities. It provides a logical representation for processes, the steps within them, and the tools or parts involved in those processes.

Classes (Types of Entities)

  • Procedure: Represents a process or method.
  • Step: Represents a step within a procedure.
  • Toolbox: Represents a container for tools.
  • Tool: Represents an individual tool.
  • Item: Represents a general item.
  • Part: Represents a part of an item.
  • Image: Represents an image associated with a step.

Object Properties (Relationships Between Entities)

  • has_step: Links a procedure to its steps.
  • has_tool: Links a toolbox to its tools.
  • has_image: Links a step to its images.
  • has_part: Links a procedure to its parts.
  • part_of: Indicates that an item or part is part of another item (transitive).
  • used_in: Links a tool to the step it is used in.
  • has_item: Links a procedure to its items.
  • has_toolbox: Links a procedure to its toolbox.
  • subprocedure: Indicates that one procedure is a subprocedure of another (transitive).

Data Properties (Attributes of Entities)

  • step_number: An integer representing the number of a step.
  • step_description: A string describing a step.
  • has_name: A string representing the name of a procedure, tool, item, or part.

Constraints

  • Procedure: Must have at least one step, exactly one toolbox, one item, one part, and one name.
  • Part, Item, Tool: Must have exactly one name.
  • Step: Must have exactly one step number and one step description.

SWRL Rules (Logical Implications)

The following rules ensure logical consistency in the ontology:

  • Rule 1-9: Ensure that a procedure has steps, toolboxes, items, and parts, and that tools are used in steps.
  • Additional Rules: Handle subprocedures, tool usage in steps, and the transitivity of the part_of relationship.

Disjoint Classes

Disjointness ensures that an entity cannot belong to two specific classes at the same time:

  • Procedure and Step: Ensures that an entity cannot be both a procedure and a step.
